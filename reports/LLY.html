Investment Thesis

HOLD

Eli Lilly is experiencing exceptional growth driven by blockbuster GLP-1 drugs Mounjaro (diabetes) and Zepbound (weight loss). Revenue surged 32% YoY to $45B, with net income more than doubling (+102%) to $10.6B. The company achieved elite profitability with 81.3% gross margin, 28.2% operating margin, and 23.5% net margin.

The GLP-1 market is massive and rapidly expanding, with obesity affecting 40%+ of US adults. Lilly is capacity-constrained rather than demand-constrained, with supply issues limiting growth potential. The company is investing heavily in manufacturing capacity, spending $18B+ on new facilities. FCF grew 122% to $7.9B despite these investments.

Valuation Concern: At 67.3x P/E and PEG ratio of 2.1, LLY is trading at a significant premium to historical pharma valuations. While growth justifies some premium, current pricing leaves limited margin of safety. Competition from Novo Nordisk (Ozempic/Wegovy) and emerging biosimilars pose risks. Debt increased 54% to $29.5B to fund capacity expansion.

Recommendation: HOLD for existing positions. The fundamentals are exceptional, but valuation is stretched. Wait for a pullback to 50x P/E (~$585) for new entry. Watch for: (1) capacity ramp timelines, (2) insurance coverage expansion, (3) clinical trial results for next-gen GLP-1s.
