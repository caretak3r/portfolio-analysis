Investment Thesis

HOLD/WATCH

Tesla faces significant headwinds in Q3 2025, with revenue declining -2.8% YoY to $69.93B and operating margins compressing sharply from 7.6% to 4.2%. The combination of aggressive price cuts, increased competition, and elevated operating expenses has led to a 46% decline in operating income YoY.

The only bright spot is free cash flow improvement (+210% to $4.80B), driven primarily by reduced capital expenditures rather than operational efficiency. With gross margins below 20% and declining, Tesla's Rule of 40 score of just 4.0% indicates a business under significant stress.

Key Concerns: Revenue decline in a growth market, margin compression despite scale, rising R&D and SG&A as percentage of revenue, and intensifying EV competition. The company needs to demonstrate pricing power recovery and volume growth in coming quarters.
